# Chapter 06 — Progression Systems

This chapter defines the authoritative structure of progression within **Too Many Coins**.

Progression in this system is **descriptive, not mechanical**. It emerges from competitive outcomes within fixed rules. No progression ladder, unlock tree, leveling system, tenure-based advantage, escalating access model, or persistent convenience progression exists.

**Persistence clarification (non-mechanical):** Competitive outcomes and history MAY persist as records (Chapter 13), but MUST NOT grant mechanical advantage, unlocks, or power in future seasons.

This chapter **does not redefine**:
- Resource properties (Chapter 04)
- Economic formulas or policy layers (Chapter 05)
- Action validation, tick order, activity enforcement, or boost mechanics (Chapter 03)
- Seasonal timing, season creation, and yearly reset timing (Chapter 07)
- Win / loss definitions and official outcome framing (Chapter 09)

If a progression vector is not explicitly defined here, it does not exist.

---

## 6.1 In-Season Progression

### 6.1.1 Definition

In-season progression is purely competitive and quantitative.

It is defined exclusively by:
- **Accumulation of Seasonal Stars**
- **Movement on the seasonal leaderboard**

Economic leverage during a season MAY influence outcomes via existing systems (no unlocks), including:
- Sigil acquisition (drop or vault purchase)
- Boost activation and stacking
- Strategic resource positioning
- Trading outcomes **(trading MAY be disabled by lifecycle and/or `gate_trading`; if disabled, it contributes nothing)**

Progress does not grant access to new mechanics.  
Progress does not unlock systems.  
Progress does not change the rule set.

All players operate on the same mechanical surface from the moment of seasonal entry. Differences in outcome emerge solely from:
- Decisions
- Timing
- Resource allocation
- Risk tolerance
- Competitive positioning

Progress equals outperforming other participants within identical constraints.

### 6.1.2 Seasonal Leaderboard Structure

Seasonal leaderboard rank is strictly ordinal.

Ranking is determined by:
1. Numeric Seasonal Star total (descending)
2. A deterministic technical tie-break key (ascending)

There is:
- No ELO system
- No weighting
- No decay
- No normalization
- No historical input
- No performance carryover

#### 6.1.2.1 Seasonal Tie-Break Key (Authoritative)

Seasonal ordering tuple:
`(SeasonalStars desc, season_tiebreak_key asc)`

Where:
- `SeasonalStars` is the player’s stored Seasonal Stars total in the season economy state.
- `season_tiebreak_key` MUST be computed server-internally as:
  - `season_tiebreak_key = HMAC_SHA256(season_seed, player_id_bytes)`

Input encoding (canonical):
- `player_id_bytes` = `player_id` encoded as **uint64 big-endian** (8 bytes).
- `season_seed` = stored **random 256-bit key bytes** (32 bytes) associated with the season.

Comparison rule:
- `season_tiebreak_key` MUST be treated as a 32-byte digest.
- Ordering by `season_tiebreak_key asc` MUST compare digest bytes lexicographically.

Collision fallback (determinism hardening):
- If (and only if) two players share identical `season_tiebreak_key` within the same season, ordering MUST fall back to `player_id asc` as a final deterministic tie-break.

Re-entry stability:
- The seasonal tie-break key MUST remain stable across re-entry within the same season because it is derived from `player_id` and `season_seed` (not anonymous join identifiers).

**Seasonal leaderboard membership and visibility are references (authoritative elsewhere):**
- **Membership predicate (who appears as a current participant):** Chapter 03 (participation predicates + join-instance terminality)
- **Identity field list and spectatorship visibility surfaces:** Chapter 08

### 6.1.3 Authoritative Seasonal Ranking Value (Owner = Chapter 06)

`authoritative_seasonal_value(P,S)` is the numeric value used for **seasonal leaderboard ordering** and for “top value == 0” guards.

**Definition (authoritative):**
- `authoritative_seasonal_value(P,S) := SeasonalStars_total(P,S)`

No other seasonal ranking value exists.

### 6.1.4 What In-Season Progression Is Explicitly NOT

The following systems do not exist within a season:
- Level systems
- Experience (XP) systems
- Skill trees
- Permanent stat growth
- Scaling action capacity
- Increased action frequency
- Tenure-based drop improvements
- Reduced trade fees over time
- Time-based cost reductions
- Loyalty ramps
- Unlock thresholds
- Gated mechanics
- Soft unlocks (tutorials/hints/recommendations) gated by tenure

Time inside a season does not grant mechanical authority. A player on the first tick and a player on the final tick operate under the same constraints. The economy changes. The rules do not.

### 6.1.5 Sigil Tier Access

Sigil tiers (I–V) represent rarity and power magnitude, not progression gates.

Access to Sigil tiers is NOT restricted by:
- Seasonal Star thresholds
- Leaderboard position
- Tenure
- Time elapsed
- Participation duration
- Performance metrics

If a Sigil exists in vault inventory, any participating player MAY purchase it, subject only to:
- Available Seasonal Stars
- Vault stock
- Deterministic validation order
- Competitive timing

Tier defines effect strength and scarcity. Tier does not define eligibility.

UI visibility:
- All tiers MUST be visible immediately (no tier visibility gating over time).

### 6.1.6 Participation Time Is Not Progression

Participation time:
- does not unlock systems
- does not modify drop rates
- does not reduce friction
- does not alter access
- does not influence pricing
- does not grant in-season advantage

Participation time is evaluative only for post-season bonus calculation. It has zero mechanical authority during live seasonal play.

Visibility rule:
- Participation time MUST NOT be shown at all during the season.

Presence is not progression.

### 6.1.7 Re-Entry and Mechanical Flatness

Re-entry into a season:
- does not unlock or alter access
- does not modify drop rates
- does not smooth or protect economic state
- does not preserve leaderboard memory
- does not influence competitive perception

Re-entry resets the player to a mechanically flat state identical to initial entry, except for preserved participation time used solely for post-season evaluation.

No continuity advantage exists.

## 6.2 Cross-Season Progression

### 6.2.1 Persisting Elements (Closed Set)

Across seasons, only the following persist:
- **Global Stars** (within the current yearly cycle)
- **Cosmetics** (meta unlocks == cosmetics only)
- **Seasonal top-three placement badges** (permanent)
- **Yearly top-ten placement badges** (permanent)

No other mechanical value survives seasonal boundaries.

The following do not persist:
- Coins
- Seasonal Stars
- Sigils
- Boost states
- Economic modifiers
- Participation time (as an active season input)
- Hoarding state
- Inflation state
- Trade configuration
- Competitive leverage of any kind
- Aggregate lifetime “stats” surfaces (totals, best finishes, seasons played counters, streaks, etc.)

Each season begins mechanically flat.

Per-season outcome/history records MAY persist as defined in Chapter 13, but MUST NOT be surfaced as aggregate progression counters or mechanical unlocks.

### 6.2.2 Global Stars as the Sole Numeric Carryover

Global Stars are the only numeric cross-season progression vector.

Global Stars are:
- cumulative within a yearly cycle
- deterministic
- strictly ordinal in leaderboard ranking
- destroyable via cosmetic purchases
- fully erased at yearly reset

There are no:
- persistent boost unlocks
- permanent Sigil tier access
- trade discounts
- passive income modifiers
- win multipliers
- veteran bonuses

Global Stars measure historical outcome, not mechanical power.

#### 6.2.2.1 Seasonal Exit Conversion Magnitude (Authoritative)

On season exit (Lock-In resolution or natural season expiration finalization):

- `GlobalStars += SeasonalStars_final` (1:1, integer)

Constraints:
- No rounding
- No scaling
- No multipliers

`SeasonalStars_final`:
- The player’s stored Seasonal Stars total at the instant the exit resolves (Lock-In) or at expiration finalization (natural season end).

### 6.2.3 Expiration Finalization Awards (Bonuses + Badges) (Non-Mechanical Reward Math)

All expiration-time awards are computed only at **natural season expiration finalization** (timing owned by Chapter 03 / Chapter 07).

Canonical sets:
- `end_finishers(S) = { P | eligible_end_snapshot(P,S) == true }` where `eligible_end_snapshot(P,S)` is defined in Chapter 03.
- `final_rank_entries(S)` is the set of ranked entries included in final season ordering (defined mechanically in Chapter 03; includes end-finishers and Lock-In entrants before cutoff).

Canonical final ordering used for outcome labels:
- The final season ordering `O` MUST be computed using the Chapter 06 ordering tuple over `final_rank_entries(S)`:
  - primary: `authoritative_seasonal_value(P,S)` (descending)
  - secondary: `season_tiebreak_key(P,S)` (ascending)
  - tertiary (collision fallback): `player_id` (ascending)

No-winner / zero-value guard:
- Let `rank1 = O[1]` be the #1 entry in the final ordering `O`.
- If `authoritative_seasonal_value(rank1,S) == 0`, then **no seasonal top-three badges** and **no placement bonus** are awarded for that season.
- Participation bonus MAY still be awarded to end-finishers.

Award sequencing (deterministic):
1. Compute final ordering `O` over `final_rank_entries(S)` using the canonical rules above.
2. Award seasonal top-three badges (6.2.4.1) using `O`, subject to the no-winner/zero-value guard.
3. Award placement bonus as Global Stars (6.2.3.2) to end-finishers only, using `O` filtered to end-finishers, subject to the no-winner/zero-value guard.
4. Award participation bonus as Global Stars to end-finishers only (6.2.3.1).
5. Apply natural exit conversion for end-finishers only: `GlobalStars += SeasonalStars_final` (see 6.2.2.1).

Non-impact guarantees:
- Participation and placement awards MUST NOT affect Seasonal Stars.
- Participation and placement awards MUST NOT affect seasonal placement ordering (ordering is computed before awards are applied).
- Participation and placement awards MUST NOT affect any in-season mechanic.

> Note (reference-only): Chapters 03 and 07 define the authoritative execution timing of expiration finalization. This chapter defines reward/badge math and award eligibility only.

#### 6.2.3.1 Participation Bonus (Magnitude)

Participation bonus is granted at natural season expiration finalization as **Global Stars**.

Definitions:
- Let `active_ticks` = the player’s total authoritative ticks spent in `Active` activity state for the season **while `participation_enabled == true`**, including across re-entry.
- Activity state semantics and `participation_enabled` semantics are defined in Chapter 03. This chapter only consumes the resulting count.

Formula (exact):
- `bonus_global_stars = min( floor(active_ticks / 3600), 56 )`

Award rule (end-finishers only):
- At expiration finalization, if `eligible_end_snapshot(P,S) == true` (Chapter 03), then:
  - `GlobalStars += bonus_global_stars`.

#### 6.2.3.2 Placement Bonus (Top 3) — Magnitudes (End-finishers only)

Placement bonus is granted at natural season expiration finalization as **Global Stars**.

Eligibility:
- Only end-finishers are eligible: `eligible_end_snapshot(P,S) == true` (Chapter 03).
- Placement selection uses the already-computed final ordering `O` filtered to end-finishers:
  - `O_end = [ P ∈ O | eligible_end_snapshot(P,S) == true ]`

Value guard:
- Placement bonus awards occur only if `authoritative_seasonal_value(O[1],S) > 0` (no-winner/zero-value guard).

Amounts (by `O_end` order):
- Rank #1: `GlobalStars += 100`
- Rank #2: `GlobalStars += 60`
- Rank #3: `GlobalStars += 40`

Edge case:
- If fewer than 3 end-finishers exist, evaluate ranks up to `min(3, |O_end|)`.

### 6.2.4 Badges (Cutoffs, Timing, and Visibility)

Badges are representational only and have zero mechanical impact.

Badges MAY be disabled by lifecycle and/or `gate_badges` (Chapter 14/15). If disabled, badge awarding MUST NOT occur and the client MUST treat badges as unavailable.

#### 6.2.4.1 Seasonal Top-Three Badge (Permanent)

Award basis (authoritative):
- Seasonal top-three badges are awarded using the final ordering `O` over `final_rank_entries(S)` (Chapter 03 + this chapter’s ordering tuple), subject to the no-winner/zero-value guard in 6.2.3.

Award rule:
- For each rank `k ∈ {1,2,3}` in the final ordering `O`:
  - Award the badge to the ranked player **only if** `authoritative_seasonal_value(P,S) > 0`.
  - If the ranked player at `k` has value `0`, **no badge is awarded for that rank**, and the badge does NOT shift to another rank.

Award count:
- Evaluate ranks up to `min(3, |final_rank_entries(S)|)`.

Timing:
- Awarded atomically as part of the expiration finalization process.

Persistence:
- Seasonal top-three badges MUST persist across yearly resets (permanent).

Visibility:
- Visible on **Profile** only.

#### 6.2.4.2 Yearly Top-Ten Badge (Permanent)

Eligible set (authoritative):
- Eligible accounts are exactly those with `GlobalStars > 0` immediately before the yearly wipe.

Award rule:
- Awarded to ranks **#1–#10** using the final computed global ordering at the instant yearly reset executes.

Award count:
- Award up to `min(10, eligible_count)` among eligible accounts at reset time.

Timing + sequencing at yearly reset (authoritative):
1. Compute final global ordering and award yearly top-ten badges using the **outgoing** `year_seed`.
2. Wipe Global Stars (set to zero for all accounts).
3. Regenerate `year_seed` for the new yearly cycle.

Visibility:
- Visible on **Profile**
- Visible on **Global leaderboard**

### 6.2.5 Cosmetics (Non-Mechanical Status + Pricing Tiers)

Cosmetic ownership has zero mechanical impact and MUST NOT affect identity visibility/anonymity rules.

Cosmetics are purchased using **Global Stars only**.

#### 6.2.5.1 Cosmetic Pricing Tier Structure (Authoritative)

All cosmetic items MUST be priced at exactly one of the following Global Star tier values:

- `10`
- `25`
- `60`
- `150`
- `400`

No other Global Star prices are valid unless this chapter is amended.

Item catalogs, specific cosmetic inventory, and presentation are not defined here; only the tier structure and currency rule are defined here.

### 6.2.6 Intentional Fragility of Status

Cross-season progression is intentionally fragile and governed by two structural principles:

1. Voluntary destruction  
   - Global Stars MAY be permanently spent on cosmetics.  
   - Spending immediately reduces leaderboard rank (if listed).

2. Scheduled erasure  
   - Global Stars are erased at yearly reset.  
   - No numeric accumulation survives beyond the yearly cycle.

This design prevents:
- Infinite power curves
- Veteran lock-in
- Permanent stratification
- Compounding advantage across years

What persists across yearly reset:
- Cosmetics
- Seasonal badges
- Yearly badges

No numeric status is permanent.

## 6.3 Global Leaderboard Progression

### 6.3.1 Ordinal Structure

Global leaderboard rank is determined strictly by:
1. Numeric Global Star total (descending)
2. Deterministic technical tie-breaker (ascending)

There is:
- No ELO system
- No hidden rating
- No decay
- No seasonal weighting
- No recency bias
- No normalization

If Player A has more Global Stars than Player B, Player A ranks higher.

### 6.3.2 Membership Rule (Visibility)

Global leaderboard membership is restricted to:
- Only accounts with `GlobalStars > 0` in the current yearly cycle.

If `GlobalStars == 0`, the account MUST be hidden from the global leaderboard.

Implication:
- Immediately after yearly reset (after the wipe), the global leaderboard becomes empty until at least one account earns Global Stars again.

Player-facing UX constraint (binding):
- “You’ll appear on the Global leaderboard after you earn your first Global Star this year.”
- “Global Stars are earned through season outcomes/Lock-In—until then, your rank is unlisted (not a failure state).”

### 6.3.3 Global Tie-Break Semantics (Authoritative)

Global ordering tuple:
`(GlobalStars desc, year_tiebreak_key asc)`

Where:
- `GlobalStars` is the current-year Global Stars total.
- `year_tiebreak_key` MUST be computed server-internally as:
  - `year_tiebreak_key = HMAC_SHA256(year_seed, player_id_bytes)`

Input encoding (canonical):
- `player_id_bytes` = `player_id` encoded as **uint64 big-endian** (8 bytes).
- `year_seed` = stored **random 256-bit key bytes** (32 bytes) for the current yearly cycle.

Comparison rule:
- `year_tiebreak_key` MUST be treated as a 32-byte digest.
- Ordering by `year_tiebreak_key asc` MUST compare digest bytes lexicographically.

Collision fallback (determinism hardening):
- If (and only if) two accounts share identical `year_tiebreak_key` within the same year, ordering MUST fall back to `player_id asc` as a final deterministic tie-break.

Reset behavior:
- Because `year_seed` regenerates at yearly reset, `year_tiebreak_key` MUST change at yearly reset even though `player_id` is stable.
- No player carries historical tie-break advantage into a new yearly cycle.

### 6.3.4 Update Timing Guarantee (Minimal Restatement)

Global leaderboard ordering MUST update **atomically** whenever a committed transaction changes Global Stars (earn or spend).

## 6.4 What Progression Is Explicitly NOT

Progression in Too Many Coins is not:
- a leveling ladder
- an XP accumulation track
- a skill tree
- a permanent stat growth system
- a veteran advantage model
- a compounding interest mechanic
- a long-term mechanical escalation curve
- an unlock-driven access hierarchy
- a tenure-based power ramp
- an achievement-based mechanical reward system

No achievements system exists whatsoever.

There is no power creep across seasons.  
There is no mechanical advantage tied to account age.  
There is no system that becomes more permissive with time.  
The mechanical surface area of the game is fixed.

### 6.4.1 Formal Progression Framing (Explanatory Only)

Progression in Too Many Coins is emergent, competitive, and sacrificial.

Emergent:
- It arises from economic outcomes and player decisions.
- It is not granted by system milestones.

Competitive:
- It exists only relative to other players.
- It has no single-player ladder.

Sacrificial:
- Status can be voluntarily reduced (cosmetic spending).
- Status is structurally erased yearly.
- Prestige requires trade-offs.

This framing reflects the mechanical realities defined above:
- Seasonal Stars determine in-season standing.
- Global Stars determine yearly standing.
- Cosmetics and badges express identity only.

Progression is an outcome, not a ladder. No additional progression model exists beyond these definitions.

End of Chapter 06.
