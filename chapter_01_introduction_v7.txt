# Chapter 01 — Introduction

## 1.1 Overview of the Game Concept

Too Many Coins is a season-based economic simulation built around a Universal Basic Income (UBI) system and a cyclical global leaderboard.

Gameplay takes place within independent, time-bounded seasons. Each season lasts **28 days**. New seasons begin every **7 days**, creating a permanent staggered structure of overlapping seasonal environments. These values are fixed for final release.

Each season functions as a closed economic ecosystem. **Coins** are the season’s currency. Players start each season join (including re-entry) with **0 Coins** and **0 Seasonal Stars**.

Players MAY spectate any season at any time, regardless of participation status. Players MAY spectate multiple seasons concurrently via multiple sessions/tabs; a single client view selects one viewing season at a time (Chapter 10). This “spectating” is **cross-season spectatorship** (Chapter 08) and is **purely observational** and grants **zero** economic effects, including but not limited to: UBI accrual, Sigil drops, trading access, vault access, Boost interaction, Seasonal Star changes, or any conversion behavior.

Players MAY economically participate in only one active season at a time. If a player is economically participating in one active season, any attempt to economically join a different active season MUST be rejected. Spectatorship remains allowed.

For purposes of this chapter, **economic participation** includes:
- UBI accrual / earnings
- Receiving Sigil drops
- Spending/earning Coins
- Spending/earning Seasonal Stars
- Purchasing Sigils from the seasonal vault
- Trading (Sigils/Coins/Sigils)
- Purchasing/activating Boosts
- Lock-In / exit actions that trigger conversion rules

**All season joins and re-entries** are subject to the same eligibility rule: a player becomes economically eligible starting on the **next authoritative server update** after the join/re-entry is accepted by the system. UI feedback MAY be immediate, but economic effects MUST NOT begin on the same update step as the join/re-entry acceptance.

Currency is generated exclusively through UBI. UBI is the **only** Coins faucet in production gameplay. UBI distributes continuously over time under server authority. The rate of distribution dynamically adjusts based on player activity state.

The only activity states are:
- **Active**
- **Idle**

Offline is **not** an activity state. Connectivity is represented separately via server online flags (e.g., `online_snapshot(T)` / `online_current`, as defined in Chapter 03), and does not create a separate UBI branch.

UBI rate changes follow the economic model defined in Chapter 05. No additional per-player “activity ramp” system exists unless explicitly defined there.

Within a season, players use Coins to compete for **Seasonal Stars**. Seasonal Stars determine placement on that season’s leaderboard and also function as a **spendable competitive resource**. Spending Seasonal Stars permanently reduces a player’s Seasonal Star total and therefore impacts their seasonal leaderboard position immediately.

Seasonal Stars MAY be spent to purchase **Sigils** from a shared seasonal Sigil vault. The vault is globally shared among all economically participating players in that season, is initialized with fresh inventory at season start, and never replenishes. In this chapter, pricing is summarized as: **Sigil prices rise as vault supply falls**. (Full pricing behavior is defined elsewhere.)

The seasonal leaderboard is dynamic throughout the season and reflects current Seasonal Star totals for all **currently economically participating** players.

When a player exits a season via **Lock-In**, they are removed from **current-participant** season surfaces (live leaderboard, participant listings, and any telemetry surfaces intended to represent current participation). However, their **latest successful Lock-In snapshot** (pre-exit Seasonal Stars and tie-break fields) remains stored and **MAY** be used for season-final ordering and outcome labels (Chapters 03, 06, 09). A Lock-In exit therefore removes the player from “who is in the season now,” but does not erase their persisted ranking record.

Upon season exit, remaining Seasonal Stars convert into **Global Stars**:
- **Lock-In (early exit):** at the moment Lock-In resolves (outside blackout), `GlobalStars += SeasonalStars_current` (1:1 integer), then `SeasonalStars = 0`.
- **Natural season end:** for players still economically participating at the committed `E−1` snapshot, expiration finalization awards any eligible end-of-season bonuses as **Global Stars**, then converts remaining Seasonal Stars 1:1 into Global Stars, then sets `SeasonalStars = 0`.

Coins do **not** convert and MUST be destroyed on any seasonal exit. No Coins, Sigils, Boosts, or other season-bound state transfers across seasonal boundaries.

Players may remain in a season until its natural conclusion or choose to exit early by **Locking In** their Seasonal Stars. Lock-In does **not** grant additive end-of-season bonuses; those bonuses exist only for players who finish the season naturally (Chapters 06, 07).

**Blackout constraint (summary):** Lock-In is unavailable during blackout; players MAY join/re-enter during blackout but MUST NOT be able to Lock-In again before expiration (Chapter 07).

Re-entry into a season is a **resource fresh start**. After exiting a season, a player MAY re-enter that same still-active season; re-entry and initial season join are mechanically identical for resources:
- 0 Coins
- 0 Seasonal Stars
- No restored Sigils
- No restored in-season boosts/modifiers

Participation-time accumulation (for time-spent bonus eligibility at natural end) is **cumulative across re-entries** within the same season (Chapter 06).
**Sigils** are temporary inventory items acquired through two paths:
1. **Drop-based distribution** during Active participation (**free**; no Seasonal Star cost)
2. **Vault purchase** using Seasonal Stars (spend reduces Seasonal Stars immediately)

Sigils may be traded between players in exchange for other Sigils or Coins (feature exists in the final design but may be disabled in certain lifecycle phases). Trade costs are paid immediately upon confirmation and are visible only to the individual player paying the cost. Trade costs **may differ per party**. Upon any seasonal exit (early Lock-In or natural end), a player’s Sigil inventory is destroyed. Sigils never persist beyond a seasonal boundary.

Sigils are consumed to purchase and activate temporary in-season **Boosts**. Boosts represent temporary competitive power that exists only within the season in which they are activated. Some Boosts affect only the activating player, while others apply globally to all **economically participating** players in that same season. Global Boosts apply to any player who is economically participating **at any time while the Boost is active**, including players who join after activation. No Boost persists beyond seasonal boundaries.

At natural season end, two additive bonuses are awarded as **Global Stars** during expiration finalization:
1. **Participation Bonus** tied to qualifying **Active** participation time within that season (Active ticks only; not Idle time)
2. A **leaderboard placement bonus** awarded to the **1st, 2nd, and 3rd** place finishers

These bonuses MUST NOT modify Seasonal Stars, and MUST NOT affect seasonal placement ordering. Placement ordering is computed from the **post-`E−1` committed snapshot** (pre-bonus), as defined in Chapter 06.

After bonuses are awarded, exit conversion applies: remaining Seasonal Stars are converted to Global Stars, and Seasonal Stars are set to 0 (Chapter 03 ordering; Chapter 06 sequencing).

Only players who remain economically participating in the season at natural end are eligible for these bonuses. Early exits via Lock-In do not receive additive bonuses.

**Global Stars** are the sole determinant of global leaderboard rank. Rank is strictly quantitative and determined by numeric ordering. Deterministic tie-breakers ensure uniquely ordered rankings at all times (Chapter 06).

The global leaderboard operates in yearly cycles consisting of **twelve consecutive season expirations**. At the end of each yearly cycle, all Global Star totals are erased and global leaderboard ranks reset. This reset is **non-negotiable** and always occurs. Permanent yearly badges are awarded to players who placed **1st through 10th** using the final computed ordering at the instant reset executes.

No public archive of prior yearly totals or full rank listings persists after reset. Only permanent badges, cosmetics, and any retained outcome facts required to support those achievements persist (Chapters 06, 09, 13).

Prestige exists in three visible layers:
1. **Seasonal prestige:** top-three seasonal placement badges awarded at natural season end
2. **Yearly prestige:** permanent badges for top-ten yearly placement at yearly reset
3. **Cosmetic prestige:** profile and interface enhancements purchased using Global Stars

Spending Global Stars permanently reduces a player’s Global Star total and may lower their rank. Cosmetics have **zero mechanical impact** and do not affect gameplay. Cosmetics persist across yearly resets. A persistent account layer exists to hold cosmetics and other permanent prestige.

All seasons are fully transparent in their economic telemetry. Spectators and participants see the same real-time seasonal economic data and the full seasonal leaderboard movement. Only **current participant count** is visible; no lifetime, total, or ex-participant counts are shown.

Seasonal identity surfaces are **non-anonymous** in final design. Seasonal leaderboard rows and season-chat attribution display `handle` only (cosmetics are profile-only and are not shown on chat or leaderboards; Chapter 08). No anonymous season identifiers exist in the final release ruleset.

Player-specific telemetry is visible only to the individual player and is not visible to others.

## 1.2 Core Game Fantasy & Player Intent

The primary player fantasy of Too Many Coins is strategic timing mastery.

Core player intent is expressed through three verbs: **Accrue**, **Spend**, **Lock In**.

**Tension slogan:** **Rank vs Power vs Safety — every season forces a trade.**

Players are rewarded for correctly judging when to accrue Seasonal Stars, when to convert seasonal prestige into temporary competitive power through Sigil purchases, and when to exit a season to secure Global recognition.

Locking in Seasonal Stars is the central expression of this mastery. It is the deliberate conversion of temporary in-season leverage into permanent Global prestige. Remaining in a season preserves opportunity for higher placement and additive bonuses but exposes the player to continued competitive pressure. Lock-In is both a commitment to certainty and an acceptance of foregone upside.

The emotional tone of the game is controlled tension under visible scarcity. Competition is transparent, calculated, and structurally fair—but never comfortable. Every decision operates within constrained resources, public rankings, limited Sigil supply, and irreversible trade-offs.

Power is temporary and seasonal. Prestige is deliberate and layered. Timing determines outcome.

---

## 1.3 Vision and Design Goals

The design vision of Too Many Coins is to create a competitive economic system in which:
1. **Structural fairness** is protected above all
2. **Timing-over-presence** defines advantage (players can compete without being online 24/7)
3. **Transparency** replaces hidden advantage
4. **Scarcity pressure** enforces meaningful trade-offs

Seasonal isolation ensures that no mechanical advantage compounds across environments. Competitive leverage must be earned repeatedly within each seasonal economy. Cross-season carryover is visual prestige only (badges and cosmetics), not mechanical power.

The seasonal Sigil vault reinforces shared scarcity and competitive access to temporary power. Players must choose between preserving seasonal rank or sacrificing Seasonal Stars to gain short-term advantage.

The yearly reset structure reinforces cyclical prestige. While Global Stars and rankings are erased, visible recognition accumulates through permanent badges and cosmetic markers. Long-term status is expressed through demonstrated placement and irreversible sacrifice, not permanent resource retention.

The system intentionally creates tension between securing prestige versus pursuing greater placement, maintaining rank versus purchasing identity, and short-term seasonal advantage versus long-term yearly positioning.

Prestige is not hoarded indefinitely through accumulation. It is demonstrated through visible achievement, calculated risk, and disciplined timing.

---

## 1.4 Target Audience

Too Many Coins is designed for players who prefer structured economic systems with strict boundaries, value strategic planning under time pressure, accept irreversible decisions and visible trade-offs, and compete for layered prestige rather than permanent mechanical dominance.

It is not designed for reflex-based gameplay, permanently escalating power systems, or idle-only participation. Idle participation is permitted, but it is intentionally suboptimal compared to Active participation.

The intended audience values calculated competition, transparent systems, cyclical prestige structures, and mastery expressed through timing discipline across both seasonal and yearly cycles.
