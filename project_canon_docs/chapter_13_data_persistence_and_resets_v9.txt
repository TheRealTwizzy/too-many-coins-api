## Chapter Draft (Version 9)

# 13 — Data Persistence and Resets

This chapter defines what game data **MUST persist**, what **MUST be destroyed**, what events **trigger resets**, and the **data integrity guarantees** required to keep outcomes deterministic and non-fraudulent.

**Scope boundary (hard):** Chapter 13 defines persistence categories, minimal required records/keys, retention windows, reset triggers, and integrity guarantees. It does **not** define economy math (Chapter 05), tick pipelines (Chapter 03/07), social system behavior (Chapter 08), or moderation workflows (Chapter 12). Where those chapters define retention numbers or behavior, they remain canonical; Chapter 13 references them.

---

## 13.0 Vocabulary and Persistence Categories

### 13.0.1 Terms (canonical)

- **Persist** — data MUST continue to exist across resets per rules; may or may not be player-visible.
- **Archive** — moved out of hot/live systems; still queryable per rule, usually read-only; not necessarily player-visible.
- **Retain-for-integrity** — kept only to prevent fraud/duplication or to prove outcomes; payload MAY be minimal/digested; not necessarily player-visible.
- **Player-visible** — MUST be fetchable through player-facing surfaces (subject to self/others/staff rules).

### 13.0.2 Canonical persistence categories (closed set)

1. **Account-Core** — immutable account identity and lifecycle state (e.g., `player_id`, created-at, status flags).
2. **Identity-Surfaces** — public/self-facing identity fields + redaction rules (handles, display name, avatar state).
3. **Progression-Persistent** — cross-season progression explicitly allowed to persist (per Chapter 06 closed set).
4. **Economy-Live (Season-Bound)** — live season economy state destroyed on exit/end (Coins, Seasonal Stars, Sigils, etc.).
5. **Outcome-History** — immutable end-of-season / end-of-year facts required to remain representable (Chapter 09 facts, “no winner” reasons, final ranks).
6. **Participation-History** — player↔season participation records needed for history + rankability (joins, Lock-Ins, end snapshots).
7. **Social-Artifacts** — friends/blocks + messaging shells subject to retention windows (owned by Chapter 08).
8. **Moderation-Artifacts** — enforcement state + evidence retention guarantees (owned by Chapter 12).
9. **Client-Local** — device/local preferences that do not reset (Chapter 10; acknowledged here only).

**Closed-set rule:** Chapter 13 makes **no persistence guarantees** for data not explicitly listed under these categories (or explicitly referenced as canonical in another chapter).

### 13.0.3 Minimal schema authority

Chapter 13 MAY define **minimal schema keys (names + uniqueness)** required to enforce determinism, retention, and integrity. It MUST NOT prescribe full storage schema.

## 13.1 What Persists Across Seasons

### 13.1.1 Account-Core (BETA/RELEASE: forever; ALPHA: wipe-override)

The following **MUST persist forever in `BETA`/`RELEASE`**.

In `ALPHA`, ops-only lifecycle wipes MAY delete gameplay state per Chapter 14; the handle registry invariants still apply.

The following MUST persist:

- `player_id` — stable, unique account identifier.
  - MUST be representable as `uint64` for tie-break encoding (see Chapter 06).
- `account_created_at` — account creation timestamp.
- `moderation_status` — moderation flags + expiry where applicable (canonical behavior in Chapter 12).
- `year_seq: int32` — authoritative monotonic yearly cycle number; increments by **+1** exactly once per yearly reset.
- `year_seed: bytes32 (or uuid)` — regenerated each `year_seq`; used for deterministic year-scoped tie-breaks if any.

**Non-existence rule:** Anything not listed above is treated as undefined/non-existent for Chapter 13 persistence guarantees.

### 13.1.2 Identity-Surfaces (forever, with redaction)

The following **MUST persist forever in `BETA`/`RELEASE`**.

In `ALPHA`, ops-only lifecycle wipes MAY delete gameplay state per Chapter 14; the handle registry invariants still apply.

The following MUST persist:

- `handle` — current handle value (may change; the field persists).
- `handle_history[]` — all historical handles + timestamps (**full history forever**).
- `last_handle_change_at` — timestamp of last successful handle change commit (self-change or admin reset); used for cooldown enforcement (Chapter 08/12).
- `profile.visibility` — profile visibility flag (public effect; Chapter 08).
- `profile.display_name_override` — staff-only redaction label (public effect; may be `null`; Chapter 08).
- `profile.avatar` — cosmetic profile avatar pointer/state (profile-only presentation; Chapter 08).
- `account.status.profile_deleted_at` — redaction timestamp (`null` or set); persists forever.

#### 13.1.2.1 Handle non-reuse (hard invariant)

- Handle non-reuse MUST be enforceable forever.
- The system MUST retain full handle history forever to enforce non-reuse.
- A handle MUST NOT be reassigned to any other account, ever.

#### 13.1.2.2 Account deletion does not exist

- **Account deletion does not exist.**
- Only **profile deletion (redaction-only)** exists.

### 13.1.3 Profile deletion (redaction-only) persistence contract

When a profile is deleted (redaction-only), the following MUST still persist and remain queryable server-side:

- **Outcome-History:** season outcomes, final ranks, no-winner reasons, badge award facts.
- **Participation-History:** participation records, Lock-In snapshots/history, end snapshots, `participation_time_total`.
- **Progression-Persistent:** cosmetics ownership, badges already awarded, `GlobalStars` (until yearly reset).
- **Moderation-Artifacts:** enforcement actions + tombstones, reports, evidence (within retention), audit trails.
- **Social-Artifacts:** friends/blocks graph and DM thread behavior as defined by Chapter 08; messages still follow retention windows.
- **Integrity artifacts:** handle history, idempotency records, finalization proofs/digests.

**Rendering rule:** Redaction changes identity rendering (e.g., `"[Removed]"`) but MUST NOT alter outcomes, ranks, badges, cosmetics ownership, or participation history facts.

**Internal reference rule:** Internal references MUST remain keyed by `player_id` everywhere (no exceptions); only rendering changes.

### 13.1.4 Progression-Persistent (Chapter 06 closed set)

Chapter 13 MUST NOT expand cross-season “progression” beyond Chapter 06’s closed set.

- Cosmetics ownership: **persists forever**, including across yearly resets. No revocation triggers exist.
- Badges: **persist forever exactly as awarded**, including if profile is redacted.
- `GlobalStars`: year-scoped; resets at yearly reset (see 13.2.3).

### 13.1.5 Outcome-History (permanent forever; player-visible)

All success/outcome facts defined in Chapter 09 are **permanent forever**.

**Visibility requirement:** Outcome-History facts MUST be player-visible in **both**:
- profile surfaces, and
- dedicated history surfaces.

**Final ranks:**
- The system MUST persist **full final ranks for all eligible ranked entries** per season.

**No-winner representability:**
- For each season and year, the system MUST persist an explicit “no winner” fact plus the canonical reason enum (Chapter 09).
- Player-visible mapping MUST show the reason enum as mapped text.

### 13.1.6 Participation-History (permanent forever)

#### 13.1.6.1 SeasonParticipationRecord (required; forever)

The system MUST maintain a `SeasonParticipationRecord` keyed by:

- **Primary key:** `(player_id, season_id)` (as required by Chapter 03)

Required fields (minimum; persist forever):

- `player_id`, `season_id`
- `first_joined_at`, `last_exit_at`
- `participation_time_total` (cumulative across re-entries; defined elsewhere)
- `end_membership` (`eligible_end_snapshot(P,S)` result at committed `E−1`)
- Latest authoritative Lock-In snapshot fields (as required by Chapter 03):
  - `lock_in_effect_tick`
  - `lock_in_snapshot_seasonal_stars`
  - `lock_in_snapshot_participation_time_total`

**Compatibility rule:** “Overwrite snapshot” remains canonical for **authoritative snapshot selection** (latest successful before cutoff), not for persistence identity.

#### 13.1.6.2 Lock-In history (successful only; forever)

The system MUST persist a complete history of **successful Lock-Ins** for each `(player_id, season_id)`.

- **Primary key:** `(player_id, season_id, lock_in_effect_tick)`
- `lock_in_effect_tick` is the canonical Lock-In identity.

Required fields (minimum):

- `player_id`, `season_id`, `lock_in_effect_tick`
- `locked_at` (timestamp) MAY be retained as convenience metadata
- `seasonal_stars_converted`
- `global_stars_credited`
- `season_snapshot_digest` (integrity payload; see 13.4.4)

**Failed Lock-In attempts:** Chapter 13 makes no persistence guarantees for failed attempts.

#### 13.1.6.3 End snapshot record (required; forever)

If `eligible_end_snapshot(P,S) == true` at committed `E−1`, the system MUST persist an end snapshot record for that player-season.

Required fields (minimum):

- `player_id`, `season_id`
- `ended_at` (timestamp) and/or `end_tick`
- `end_snapshot_seasonal_stars` (or canonical final value used for ranking)
- `final_rank`
- `entry_tiebreak_hash` (hash of the tie-break outputs used; see 13.4.4)

#### 13.1.6.4 Authoritative history precedence (player-season)

1. If `eligible_end_snapshot == true`: the **end snapshot is authoritative** for:
   - the season’s final outcome label(s),
   - the player’s final rank,
   - natural-end-only bonuses and badges.
2. Lock-Ins remain visible as a separate **Lock-In History list** and do not replace the end snapshot as the season’s final outcome record.
3. If `eligible_end_snapshot == false`: the **latest successful Lock-In before cutoff** remains authoritative for rankability/outcome representation for that player-season.

### 13.1.7 Social-Artifacts (reference-only; persistence requirements)

- Social artifacts (friends, blocks, chat, DMs) are canonical in Chapter 08.
- Friends graph and blocks graph MUST persist across seasons and yearly reset.
- Global chat count limit (“last 200”), DM retention (30 days), season chat deletion at season end, and other social retention rules are enforced per Chapter 08.

### 13.1.8 Moderation-Artifacts (reference-only; persistence requirements)

- Moderation actions and evidence retention are canonical in Chapter 12.
- Moderation enforcement state MUST NOT be cleared by yearly reset.
- Message removal tombstones persist (tombstone record); underlying content retention follows Chapter 12 evidence rules.

### 13.1.9 Client-Local (acknowledgement only)

Client-local preferences persist on device per Chapter 10. Chapter 13 does not define server reset behavior for Client-Local items.

### 13.1.10 Player-visible history surfaces (required set)

The product MUST provide player-visible surfaces capable of exposing the required persistent facts, including:

- `Season History`
- `Season Leaderboards (Final)`
- `Lock-In History`
- `Year History`
- `Badges`
- `Cosmetics`

**Participation listing:** The player MUST be able to view ended seasons they participated in even if they never Lock-In’d and were not end-eligible. Minimum row fields:

- `season_id` (or label), `participation_time_total`, `status` (Participated / Not Eligible), `final_outcome_label` (if any)

**Profile visibility vs leaderboards (hard rule):**
- `profile.visibility != PUBLIC` MUST NOT erase a player’s presence on final season leaderboards.
- Profile visibility affects profile-page access; leaderboards remain globally visible.
- Identity rendering on leaderboards follows identity-label rules; redacted profiles render as `"[Removed]"`.

**Year History constraints (no public archive):**
- Multi-year `Year History` is allowed but LIMITED.
- For past years (after reset), `Year History` MUST NOT show:
  - prior `GlobalStars` totals,
  - full yearly rank listings,
  - numeric year standings beyond what a badge already implies.
- For past years, `Year History` MAY show only:
  - `year_seq`,
  - earned yearly badges (e.g., “Top-10”),
  - optional non-numeric label (e.g., “Placed Top-10”),
  - award timestamp.
- For the **current year**, the player MAY see current-year state as it accrues (including current `GlobalStars` balance).

## 13.2 What Is Destroyed

### 13.2.1 Season-bound economy destruction (Lock-In exit; player-scoped)

On `lock_in_exit`, the system MUST destroy all **Economy-Live (Season-Bound)** state for that player in that season, including:

- Coins (seasonal) → set to `0`
- Seasonal Stars → converted per Chapter 03, then set to `0`
- Sigils → set to `0`
- active boosts / boost timers / player-owned system modifiers
- open trade offers, pending trades, reservations/holds
- any season-bound inventory-like state not explicitly persistent

This destruction is **never recoverable**.

### 13.2.2 Natural season end destruction (season-scoped)

At `season_expiration_finalization`, the system MUST destroy all remaining **Economy-Live (Season-Bound)** state for that season, including:

- all remaining Coins / Seasonal Stars / Sigils
- all boosts/system events and their timers
- all trade offers/pending trades/reservations
- all other season-bound live economy state

While doing so, the system MUST preserve:

- Outcome-History (facts + final ranks + “no winner” reasons)
- Participation-History (records + snapshots + Lock-In history)

### 13.2.3 Yearly reset destruction (global)

Ownership note:
- Timing authority: Chapter 07.
- Resource-facing effects: Chapter 04 and Chapter 06.
- Persistence ordering + integrity guarantees: this chapter.

At `yearly_reset`, the system MUST:

- wipe `GlobalStars` → `0` for all accounts
- destroy yearly standings/ranks as a **public** concept (no public archive of totals/rank lists after reset)
- destroy year-scoped aggregates used only for that year (if any exist)
- rotate `year_seed` and increment `year_seq` (see 13.3.3)

The system MUST preserve:

- cosmetics ownership (BETA/RELEASE: forever; ALPHA: may be wiped by Chapter 14 wipes)
- badges (BETA/RELEASE: forever; ALPHA: may be wiped by Chapter 14 wipes)
- season history / outcome history (BETA/RELEASE: forever; ALPHA: may be wiped by Chapter 14 wipes)
- moderation/enforcement state (per Chapter 12)

### 13.2.4 Destruction mechanisms (mixed by artifact)

- **Balances/resources:** MUST become deterministically unreachable as live state (e.g., set-to-zero + close).
- **High-volume ephemeral artifacts** (offers/reservations): MAY be hard deleted.
- **Required proofs/snapshots:** MUST be archived/retained-for-integrity (not destroyed).

### 13.2.5 No recovery (hard rule)

Season-bound economy state destroyed by Lock-In exit or natural season end is **never recoverable**, including via staff intervention.

## 13.3 Reset Triggers

### 13.3.1 Closed set of reset triggers (canonical)

Chapter 13 recognizes only the following reset/retention triggers:

- `season_join`
- `lock_in_exit`
- `season_expiration_finalization`
- `yearly_reset` (executed within finalization of the 12th season expiration boundary)
- `lifecycle_wipe` (ALPHA-only, ops-only; Chapter 14; gameplay state may be destroyed)
- `beta_entry_wipe` (Alpha → Beta migration wipe; Chapter 14)
- `profile_delete` (redaction-only)
- `retention_expiry_dm`
- `retention_expiry_evidence`
- `retention_expiry_season_end_ack`
- `retention_compaction_global_chat_count_limit`
- `season_chat_delete_on_season_end`

**In-product manual/admin-triggered resets do not exist.** Ops-only lifecycle wipes exist in `ALPHA` per Chapter 14 and are treated as explicit reset triggers here.

### 13.3.2 Natural season end ordering (atomic settlement)

Natural season end ordering is defined by Chapter 03 (authoritative execution order).

Persistence requirement (binding):

- The Chapter 03 natural-end settlement MUST be treated as one logical atomic settlement from a persistence standpoint.
- This chapter defines what MUST be persisted and what MUST be destroyed/retained, but MUST NOT redefine the settlement execution ordering.


### 13.3.3 Yearly reset trigger, ordering, and counters

#### 13.3.3.1 Trigger moment

`yearly_reset` MUST execute during expiration finalization of the season that constitutes the **12th consecutive season expiration** of the current yearly cycle.

#### 13.3.3.2 Canonical ordering for the 12th season finalization that triggers yearly reset

1. Freeze last-season end snapshot at committed `E−1` (season 12).
2. Compute last-season final ranks + tie-breaks; persist final leaderboard.
3. Resolve “No Winner” (if applicable) + persist reason.
4. Award last-season natural-end-only bonuses + award season-scoped badges.
5. Convert last-season remaining Seasonal Stars → `GlobalStars` (natural end conversion).
6. Compute outgoing-year global ordering (Top-10) using outgoing `year_seed` and post-step-5 `GlobalStars` balances.
7. Award yearly Top-10 badges based on step 6.
8. Wipe `GlobalStars` → `0` (yearly reset).
9. Increment `year_seq` (+1).
10. Regenerate `year_seed` for the new `year_seq`.
11. Cleanup/close season-bound live economy state for season 12 (per Chapter 04 destruction rules).
12. Create `season_end_ack` records (non-blocking; retention per Chapter 11).

#### 13.3.3.3 UI-visible `GlobalStars` after yearly reset

If a player is participating in an active season when yearly reset happens:

- `GlobalStars` MUST snap to `0` immediately in server-authoritative responses (no reconnect required).

### 13.3.4 Retention evaluation time authority

Retention expiry evaluation MUST use server-authoritative wall-clock UTC:

- `now_utc` is authoritative for retention windows (not client time).

## 13.4 Data Integrity Guarantees

### 13.4.1 Idempotency (required operations + uniqueness keys)

The following operations MUST be idempotent and protected by uniqueness at the persistence layer:

- **Lock-In conversion credit:** unique by `(player_id, season_id, lock_in_effect_tick)`
- **Natural season-end conversion credit:** MUST apply at most once per `(player_id, season_id)`
- **Season natural-end bonus award:** MUST apply at most once per `(player_id, season_id, bonus_type)`
- **Season badge award:** unique by `(player_id, season_id, badge_type)`
- **Yearly Top-10 badge award:** unique by `(player_id, year_seq, badge_type)` *(where `year_seq` is the outgoing year being awarded)*
- **Season finalization step checkpoints:** unique by `(season_id, checkpoint)` where `checkpoint` is a stable step-name enum (e.g., `FREEZE_E_MINUS_1`, `COMPUTE_RANKS`, `AWARD_SEASON_BADGES`, `CONVERT_STARS`, `AWARD_YEAR_BADGES`, `WIPE_GLOBAL_STARS`, etc.)
- **Yearly reset run:** MUST be single-execution per outgoing `year_seq` (enforced by uniqueness on the yearly-reset verification record)
- **Profile delete (redaction):** idempotent set for `(player_id)` (single active redaction timestamp)
- **Moderation action application:** unique by `(moderation_action_id)`

### 13.4.2 Finalization retries (exactly-once externally)

Expiration finalization MUST provide **exactly-once external effects** via step checkpoints:

- If a finalization job retries after partial failure, already-completed checkpoints MUST no-op safely.
- The system MUST NOT double-credit conversions, bonuses, or badges.

### 13.4.3 Yearly reset atomicity (no partial state)

Yearly reset MUST be externally atomic:

- It MUST never be observable as partially applied.
- Implementation MAY use a single DB transaction or multi-step checkpointing, but MUST serialize execution and guarantee all-or-nothing externally.

### 13.4.4 Retain-for-integrity verification artifacts (minimum payloads; forever)

Chapter 13 guarantees minimal verification artifacts exist; audit/recompute procedures (if any) are defined by the audit owner (Chapter 15).

#### 13.4.4.1 Season finalization verification record (retain forever)

- `season_id`, `finalized_at`
- `final_order_hash` (hash of the full ordered list of `(player_id, final_rank)` plus tie-break outputs)
- `no_winner_reason` (if any)

Per-entry minimums to support integrity (retain forever):

- raw fields:
  - `season_id`
  - `player_id`
  - `final_rank`
  - `end_snapshot_seasonal_stars` (or canonical final value used for ranking)
  - `lock_in_effect_tick` for rankable lock-in entrants (if rankability depends on it)
  - `season_seed` (if any exists for tie-break; owned by ranking/audit chapters)
- hashed fields:
  - `entry_tiebreak_hash` (hash of tie-break *outputs* used for that entry)

**Note:** Chapter 13 MUST NOT claim “verify later” from a digest unless canonical serialization and tie-break output definitions are provided by the ranking/audit owner (Chapter 09/15). Chapter 13 retains raw minimums + hashes for tamper-evidence.

#### 13.4.4.2 Yearly reset verification record (retain forever; server-side only)

- `old_year_seq`, `new_year_seq`, `reset_at`
- `old_year_seed`, `new_year_seed`
- `year_top10_hash` (hash of ordered Top-10 used for yearly badges)

#### 13.4.4.3 Badge award verification record (retain forever)

- `player_id`, (`season_id` or `year_seq`), `badge_type`, `awarded_at`
- `basis_ref` (e.g., `final_rank` or eligibility flag)
- `award_digest` (hash of the basis payload)

### 13.4.5 Consistency invariants (must never be violated)

- No negative balances (Coins, Seasonal Stars, `GlobalStars`).
- A `(player_id, season_id)` MUST have at most one authoritative end snapshot.
- Each Lock-In credit applies at most once per `(player_id, season_id, lock_in_effect_tick)`.
- Natural-end credits/bonuses/badges apply at most once per their uniqueness keys.
- Handle non-reuse is absolute (no two accounts ever share a handle, including historical).
- Profile redaction MUST NOT alter outcome history or participation history facts.
- A finalized season MUST have an immutable final leaderboard record OR an explicit “Unavailable” record with a `reason_code` from Chapter 15’s closed reason-code catalog.
- Moderation enforcement state MUST NOT be cleared by yearly reset.

### 13.4.6 Deterministic history ordering key (`history_event_seq`)

The system MUST maintain a per-player monotonic history ordering key:

- `history_event_seq: int64` monotonic per `player_id`
- allocated by atomic per-player increment at write time
- ordering is the order of successful commits for that player (ties broken by transaction commit order)
- never resets, including yearly reset

Event types that MUST allocate a new `history_event_seq`:

- Lock-In success (each successful Lock-In, keyed by `lock_in_effect_tick`)
- Natural season-end settlement for the player (their season outcome becomes final)
- Season badge award (if stored separately from settlement, allocate a seq for the award record)
- Yearly Top-10 badge award
- Profile redaction applied (`account.status.profile_deleted_at` set)
- Moderation enforcement state change affecting account status (mute/ban start, expire, lift)

### 13.4.7 Retention expiry behavior (deterministic externally)

Retention systems MAY compact asynchronously, but MUST satisfy deterministic external behavior:

- DM messages (>30 days): MUST be unavailable via standard retrieval once expired; physical deletion MAY occur asynchronously.
- Evidence (>90 days): content MUST be unavailable once expired; physical deletion MAY occur asynchronously (canonical retention in Chapter 12).
- `season_end_ack` (>180 days): server MAY delete/compact internally; externally, late ACK MUST be a deterministic no-op success (canonical behavior in Chapter 11).
- Global chat count limit: to enforce “last 200,” oldest messages beyond the last 200 MUST be deleted/dropped immediately (canonical behavior in Chapter 08).

### 13.4.8 Missing/corrupt historical data (deterministic failure surface)

If historical data required for a view is missing or corrupt:

- The system MUST return a deterministic “Unavailable” response with a stable `reason_code`.
- The canonical `reason_code` taxonomy is owned by Chapter 15; Chapter 13 references it and does not define it.
- The system MUST NOT attempt “best effort reconstruction” unless Chapter 15 explicitly defines a recompute pathway.
