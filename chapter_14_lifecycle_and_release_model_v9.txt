# Chapter 14 — Lifecycle and Release Model

This chapter defines the lifecycle phases (`ALPHA`, `BETA`, `RELEASE`), what each phase permits/forbids, deterministic phase transition contracts, and a closed set of constitutional invariants.

**Authority split (binding):**
- Chapter 14 owns lifecycle policy and phase transition contracts.
- Chapter 11 owns ServerMode semantics.
- Chapter 15 owns feature-gate catalogs, reason-code catalogs, and audit schemas.
- Chapters 12–13 own moderation authority and persistence/reset semantics.

All phase/flag changes become effective at the next tick boundary (Chapter 03/11/15 alignment).

---

## 14.0 Global Rules

- `lifecycle_phase ∈ {ALPHA, BETA, RELEASE}` is a single global server-authoritative value.
- Any change MUST emit an immutable audit record (Chapter 15).
- Phase, ServerMode, and feature-gate flips MUST NOT take effect mid-tick.

---

## 14.1 Alpha Assumptions (Summary)

Alpha permits:
- invite-limited access
- feature gating of incomplete systems
- wipes (with audit + retained integrity chain)

Alpha forbids:
- monetization
- per-user preferential interventions
- silent competitive rule changes without versioning

---

## 14.2 Beta Transition Rules (Summary)

Alpha → Beta transition MUST:
- drain and settle already-accepted actions under pre-transition snapshots
- enter `MAINTENANCE_LOCKDOWN` for a hard-stop while migrating
- execute `beta_entry_wipe` (per 14.2.1; durability rules in Chapters 13/15)
- flip `lifecycle_phase = BETA` effective next tick
- operate under the Chapter 07 deterministic schedule (no phase override exists)

---

## 14.3 Final Release Assumptions (Summary)

Release stability bar:
- determinism-critical rule changes MUST occur only at season boundaries
- mid-season retroactive outcome corrections are forbidden except security/exploit fixes, and only at a season boundary

Data permanence promises (policy-level; details in Chapter 13):
- handle history (non-reuse) persists
- cosmetics entitlements persist (unless Alpha wipe)
- badges and outcome history remain renderable forever
- Coins/Seasonal Stars are season-scoped and non-persistent
- Global Stars are year-scoped and reset at yearly reset

Region-specific privacy/legal behavior:
- The service MAY enforce region-specific privacy rights that require stronger identity redaction.
- Competitive integrity MUST be preserved (outcomes remain renderable even if identities are redacted).

---

## 14.4 What Will Never Change (Constitutional Rules)

1. Single global lifecycle phase exists (`ALPHA`, `BETA`, `RELEASE`).
2. Economy-affecting and governance-affecting operations MUST be auditable and replay-safe.
3. Staff MUST NOT directly edit individual competitive outcomes.
4. Competitive acceptance/rejection MUST be deterministic under replay.
5. Idempotency replay safety MUST exist for side-effecting actions.
6. Season schedule is fixed in `BETA` and `RELEASE` (Chapter 07).

---

## 14.5 Account Erasure / Deletion (Availability by Lifecycle Phase)

**Binding rule:** Full account erasure/deletion does not exist as an in-product system.

Across all lifecycle phases:
- Only **profile deletion (redaction-only)** exists (Chapters 12–13).
- Handle non-reuse remains enforced forever (Chapter 13).
- Outcome history remains renderable; identities may be redacted to `"[Removed]"` (Chapters 12–13).

If region-specific law requires a stronger deletion-like outcome:
- It MUST be implemented as a stricter redaction policy that preserves competitive integrity and handle non-reuse.
- Any such policy MUST be explicitly documented and audited (Chapter 15), and MUST NOT be exposed as a player-initiated “delete account” button unless Chapter 12/13 are amended to define it deterministically.
